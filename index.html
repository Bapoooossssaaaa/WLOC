Short Birth Story

Kael Nicholas Charles was born on August 12, 1986, in Fairfax, VA, to Thomas and Ellie Charles in an upper-class home. He had a brother named Reese who was in college at the time of his birth. His father was a retired GS-14 Logistics Officer for the U.S. Department of Defense, and his mother was a Public School Administrator in Fairfax County; both laid their years of knowledge and work onto him throughout his younger life. During his childhood, he didn’t see his brother too often, even after he finished college, nor did his parents talk about him much. Kael was saddened that he had never once gotten to see his older brother, which laid weight on him throughout his life. His father was mostly around and gave him knowledge of his days in the Department of Defense, especially regarding what he hated about his work.

Schooling/Work Experience

Kael attended Woodson High School and was keen on becoming a part of the school’s Student Government. He succeeded in this and even served as the President of Student Government during his junior year of high school. Academically, Kael was exceedingly easy to work with and did well in all of the classes he attended—some honors, some accelerated. After graduating high school in 2004, he was able to get a scholarship to George Mason University, earning a Master’s in Organization Development & Knowledge Management by 2010. He spent the next decade working for a private sector major defense contractor, Booz Allen Hamilton, in McLean, VA. He specialized in internal auditing and human resources for classified government projects. In 2016, after five years of strategic operations, Kael obtained his Senior Certified Professional credential from the Society for Human Resource Management. He also notably spearheaded a "Compliance Overhaul" for a 500-person logistics division, restructuring wage-and-hour auditing processes to eliminate legal liabilities before a federal review.

Foundation Discovery & Recruitment

By 2020, near the end of his contract, he caught the eye of an SCP Foundation Agent working deep cover in Booz Allen Hamilton: one Alex V., disguised as a Junior IT Support Technician, but in reality, a member of the Foundation’s Intelligence Network. He wasn't there to recruit Kael initially; he was embedded in Booz Allen Hamilton to monitor a different executive suspected of selling classified logistics data to the Chaos Insurgency. That Senior Executive also fell under Kael's scrutiny during one of Kael’s audits. Kael was able to determine he was using a "Ghost Employee" scheme, creating fake employees on the payroll to funnel cash.

Kael determined that if he flagged the account publicly, the executive would likely panic and purge the servers, destroying evidence and damaging the company. Instead, he opted to quietly quarantine the specific server cluster under the guise of a "Payroll System Update," and he physically walked down to the server room to authorize a hard lock on the drive. Afterward, he invited said Executive to a private meeting. He placed a single file on the desk: a pre-written Resignation Letter and a Non-Disclosure Agreement. He gave the Executive 10 minutes to sign both and leave the building, or else a special someone playing with company money would be reported to the FBI.

Alex, watching via the network, realized Kael had inadvertently trapped the Foundation, too. When Kael locked the server to catch the thief, he locked the Agent out of his backdoor. It mattered little to the agent; the work was practically done for them. Kael was noted by the Foundation to have prioritized containment and silence instead of panicking or seeking glory. He effectively contained a breach and neutralized the threat without the public or even the wider company knowing. Two days after the Executive "retired for personal reasons," the Junior IT Tech, Alex, stopped by Kael’s office to "fix his email archive." While he worked on Kael’s terminal, Alex stopped for a moment, looked at Kael, and said:

"Most people would have called the cops, Mr. Charles. But you knew that a police investigation would tank the stock price and lose the contract. You buried the body to save the house. We need people who know how to bury things."

He then left a card that had a phone number on it, and three letters: S.C.P.

Kael made the smart move a day later and called the number. The man who answered spoke as if he had years of knowledge. “Meet me at the Iwo Jima Memorial. North bench. Midnight. Come alone, or don't come at all.”

Kael indeed arrived at midnight and saw a man in a suit and fedora sitting alone with a cigar in his mouth. The man laid a folder on the bench and left.

The folder was substantially big, containing pages of questions about him and pages of files matching him and his identity, as if they had been watching his every move for years. At the bottom of the stack was a single, crisp document stamped with the seal of the Administrative Department. It listed no salary, only a Provisional Clearance Level and a directive: 'Report to Dulles International Airport, Private Hangar 4, at 0600 hours. Do not return home. Do not say goodbye. Your work begins now.' Before he closed the folder and got up, he saw a sticky note taped to the folder, and the words on it felt like the words he’d been wanting to hear all his years: ‘You want to see your brother after all these years, make the right choice.’

Reese had actually been working for the Foundation after college and seemed to have made quite a name for himself as the Site Director for Site-15. Kael was able to gain a spot serving under him and was able to converse with him plenty for the next four years of his employment in the Foundation’s Administrative Department. He didn’t do anything too important, just managing personnel files and paychecks, as well as dealing with concerns whenever necessary.

LIABILITY WAIVER

He also looked at the schedules for researchers working with dangerous SCPs and realized most accidents happened when staff were tired.
